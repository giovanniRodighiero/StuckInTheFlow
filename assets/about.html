Something about who we are and what we do.

What is Stuck in The Flow ?

The Stuck in the Flow project originated from three friends and their will to challenge themselves on the task of building smartphone apps.

In the summer of 2013, Giovanni, Giulio and Mattia, pushed by the desire to work on something real after years of studying, decided to venture in the world of app development. After little time the idea of Quick Algebra was born, which marked the beginning of Stuck in the Flow.

The name is of course a tribute to the famous forum Stuckoverflow without which we would not have gone very far, being often quite literally stuck in the flow of ideas and code.

How are our apps born ?

Quick Algebra

The first appication we made. The three of us all worked together on it. The idea was to create a simple and fast tool in order to simplify our studying of linear algebra. It has been released in two versions, one is free, ad supported, and has limited functionality, whereas the other is paid and does not feature any ad.

For more informations about Quick Algebra Free (free version), jump to the page Quick Algebra Free.

For more informations about Quick Algebra (complete version), jump to the page Quick Algebra.

Quick Fit

Giulio made this app in order to help his course mates at physics department to perform fast regressions on measured dats. A pity Giulio and his mates use Windows Phone while the app is available (for now ...) just for Android systems.

For more informations about Quick Fit, jump to the page Quick Fit.

Simple Bubble Shooter

Very simple game made by Giovanni, it borns as a "final exercise" after his study of the Unity development platform, focusing on the 2D part of that. The game was developed entirely throught the IDE provided by Unity and optimised for the release on smartphones running Android.

For more informations about Simple Bubble Shooter, jump to the page Simple Bubble Shooter.
